Orient yourself first

The college's /business/news section is press and announcements (rankings, awards, board appointments, construction) and is excluded here as requested. The audience-facing blog is three evergreen libraries, one each for the Online MBA, Online MSBA, and MS in Accounting - the three graduate programs this campaign drives leads for. Coverage of the priority programs is genuinely strong. The real openings are the cross-program questions buyers ask that none of the three hubs answer: cost and financing, admissions specifics, and program comparisons - plus one program-specific exception, the brand-new MSABTA hybrid, which has a page but no blog content yet.

03

Dead zones worth filling

Ranked by audience demand against current coverage. Each gap pairs the missing topic with a starter post we would actually write to begin closing it.

Gap 01

Cost, tuition & financing

The audience repeatedly searches "mba program cost," "online mba cost," and "mba scholarships." The blog talks about ROI but never the actual price or how to pay for it.

A piece we'd write
"How Much Does an Online MBA Cost? (And How to Pay for It)"
Generic question up top for reach: the real price range and the levers - scholarships, employer reimbursement, federal aid - that lower it, with Kent's numbers as the worked example and CTA.
Branded companion: "Kent State Online MBA: Tuition, Fees & Aid, Explained"
Why now - high-intent, decision-stage queries with strong commercial value ($19-25 CPC). A transparent cost, aid, and scholarship guide catches buyers at the moment of hesitation.
Gap 02

GMAT waiver & no-GMAT entry

"mba gmat waiver" and "mba programs no gmat" recur as high-intent searches. This is often the single biggest objection for working professionals.

A piece we'd write
"Do You Need the GMAT for an MBA? Waivers and No-GMAT Options, Explained"
Answers the universal objection - who qualifies for a waiver and how to request one - then drops in Kent's specific criteria to convert.
Branded companion: "Does Kent State's Online MBA Require the GMAT?"
Why now - a clear "do you need the GMAT?" post removes a top barrier and pulls comparison shoppers.
Gap 03

Comparisons beyond online vs. full-time

The audience compares "MBA vs Masters in Finance," "MBA vs MS in Business Analytics," and "MBA vs EMBA." The blog only answers full-time vs. online.

A piece we'd write
"MBA vs. MSBA: Which Master's Fits Your Career?"
A high-demand generic comparison; Kent's MBA and MSBA become the real-world example, routing readers to the right fit.
Follow-up: "MBA vs. Master's in Finance: How to Choose When You Love the Numbers"
Why now - the college owns both MBA and MSBA, so a direct comparison serves the prospect and sends them to the right program.
Gap 04

Finance-specific content

The audience self-describes as "financial," "investment," "portfolio," and "financial analyst," yet nothing on the blog speaks to finance careers or the finance curriculum.

A piece we'd write
"From Analyst to Decision-Maker: How an MBA Reshapes a Finance Career"
Speaks directly to the analysts, advisors, and portfolio professionals who make up this audience's own bios.
Follow-up: "5 Finance Roles You Can Target with an MBA, and the Skills Each Demands"
Why now - content aligned to who the audience already is converts better than generic MBA content.
Gap 05

Admissions specifics: essays & interviews

"mba essay questions," "business school interview tips," and "mba application timeline" all show steady demand. One general process post exists; the high-intent specifics don't.

A piece we'd write
"How to Answer the 'Why an MBA?' Essay (With Examples That Work)"
A bottom-funnel, low-competition piece that helps applicants clear the final hurdle in Kent's own voice.
Follow-up: "Your MBA Admissions Interview: 8 Questions to Prepare For"
Why now - these are bottom-funnel, low-competition queries a single school can realistically win.
Targeted opening · inside a strength

The new MSABTA hybrid has a program page but no blog yet

Accounting overall is well covered by the MSA blog, so this is not a dead zone. But the college's newest flagship - the MS in Accounting, Business Technology and Analytics - is a separate, brand-new degree (accounting plus Python, R, machine learning, and AI). It has a live program page and zero blog content of its own, and it lands right on this audience's blended accounting-and-analytics intent and the employer demand for analytics skills on top of accounting knowledge.

A piece we'd write
"Accounting + Analytics: The Hybrid Master's Employers Are Asking For"
Generic title for reach; MSABTA is the worked example, framed around the accounting-plus-analytics skillset employers now want.
Branded companion: "Inside Kent State's MSABTA: Where Accounting Meets Python, R & AI"
Why now - a brand-new, stated 2026 priority program with zero blog support, perfectly aligned to who this audience already is.

Method & caveats

Method. Site crawled with Screaming Frog from www.kent.edu/business under a strict /business URL scope (430 unique business pages out of 4,350 site-wide HTML pages). News and press URLs were excluded per request. The crawler was bot-blocked (HTTP 403) on most blog posts, so the corpus was completed by fetching the Online MBA, MSBA, and MS in Accounting blog hubs directly. Audience signals come from the SparkToro report "Business degree seekers and MBA program researchers" - search keywords, bio keywords, AI-assistant prompt topics, and the platform-affinity and channel data behind Figure 03. Program priorities for 2026 (Online MBA, Online MSBA, and the new MSABTA) reflect the current campaign focus.

Reading the plot. Demand and coverage scores are an analyst blend of SparkToro affinity and volume against post counts. Treat marker positions as directional, not precise coordinates.

One caveat. Because the live blog hubs rotate, a handful of older posts may exist beyond the 42 mapped here. The thematic conclusions hold regardless.
